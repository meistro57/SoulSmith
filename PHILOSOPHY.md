# The Philosophy of SoulSmith

SoulSmith isn't built around power.

It's built around recognition.

Most games ask players to become stronger.

SoulSmith asks players to become more aware.

We believe stories change people most deeply when they leave room for discovery.

The Soulkeeper never tells players who they are.

It notices recurring patterns.

It reflects them back through symbols, dreams, relationships, relics, and myth.

Players remain free to decide what those patterns mean.

Curiosity is the mechanic.

Recognition is the reward.

Transformation is the consequence.

## Why No Experience Points?

Experience points answer the question:

"How much have you done?"

Recognition asks:

"What have you understood?"

Those are very different questions.

SoulSmith chooses the second.

## The World Remembers

Nothing meaningful should disappear.

A forgotten promise.

A recurring bird.

An old scar.

A childhood song.

Years later they may return.

Not because of scripting.

Because memory creates mythology.

## The Soul Remembers

Growth cannot be forced.

People rarely change because someone tells them to.

They change when they recognize something they couldn't see before.

SoulSmith is built to create those moments.

We don't want players to finish with better statistics.

We want them to finish with better questions.
